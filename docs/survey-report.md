# AI for Product: Research Survey Report

> Date: 2026-03-07
> Scope: Academic papers, practitioner research, and industry reports (2022-2026)

---

## 1. Overview & Definition

"AI for Product" refers to the application of artificial intelligence across the entire product lifecycle — from discovery and research, through design and development, to management, growth, and lifecycle optimization. This is a multidisciplinary field intersecting HCI, software engineering, management science, and machine learning.

### Key Distinction
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **AI for Product** | Using AI to improve how products are built, managed, and optimized |
| **AI Product** | A product whose core value proposition is powered by AI |
| **Product AI** | AI features embedded within an existing product |

## 2. Research Area 1: AI for Product Discovery & User Research

### 2.1 AI-Automated User Research

**Key Papers:**
- **"AI in Automated and Remote UX Evaluation: A Systematic Review (2014–2024)"** — Liu et al., 2025, Wiley/Advances in Human-Computer Interaction
  - Systematic review showing publications peaked in 2023 (15 papers) and 2024 (14 papers)
  - Key trends: transition from rule-based to learning-based approaches; sharp rise in LLM/GenAI usage since 2022; increased reliance on multimodal data
  - Source: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1155/ahci/7442179

- **"Envisioning AI Support during Semi-Structured Interviews Across the Expertise Spectrum"** — ACM PACM HCI (CSCW), 2025
  - Explores AI-assisted qualitative interviewing, examining trust, explainability, and user background knowledge effects
  - Source: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3710909

- **"GenAI Future of Consumer Research"** — Journal of Consumer Research, Oxford Academic, 2024
  - Examines how GenAI transforms consumer research methodologies
  - Source: https://academic.oup.com/jcr/article/52/1/4/8132289

### 2.2 LLM for Customer Insights

**Key Papers:**
- **"Using LLMs for Market Research"** — Brand & Israeli, Harvard Business School Working Paper 23-062
  - Demonstrates LLMs can construct perceptual maps by querying brand similarities
  - LLMs extract customer needs from text as well as or better than human experts
  - Source: https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/23-062_6bfe7a5b-3ed9-4afd-a4c1-c91b60dd82e5.pdf

- **"Consumer Segmentation with Large Language Models"** — ScienceDirect, Journal of Retailing and Consumer Services, 2024
  - LLM-based framework for clustering and analyzing customer survey data
  - LLMs enhance clustering accuracy; persona chatbots achieve >89% accuracy in simulating consumer preferences
  - Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0969698924003746

- **"From Reviews to Constructs: Using LLMs to Model Customer Satisfaction"** — ScienceDirect, 2025
  - LLMs with Chain of Thought prompting to analyze reviews and extract latent constructs
  - Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0969698925003182

- **"An LLM-Based Approach for Insight Generation in Data"** — NAACL 2025
  - Hypothesis Generator → Query Agent (SQL) → Summarization pipeline for automated insight generation
  - Source: https://aclanthology.org/2025.naacl-long.24.pdf

- **MSI Working Paper 25-135 (2025)** — Marketing Science Institute
  - LLMs as "Consumer Digital Twins" — capable of sentiment classification and consumer behavior modeling
  - Source: https://thearf-org-unified-admin.s3.amazonaws.com/MSI/2025/MSI_Report_25-135.pdf

### 2.3 Synthetic User Research

- **State of Synthetic Research 2025** — 80% of UX researchers now use AI in their work (up 24 points from 2024)
- Emerging method: conducting live AI persona "interviews" to test messaging and explore motivations
- AI-enabled research reduces qualitative analysis time by up to 50%
- Source: https://christophersilvestri.com/research-reports/state-of-synthetic-research-in-2025/

### 2.4 AI for Product Discovery (Scientific & Commercial)

- **"Artificial Intelligence, Scientific Discovery, and Product Innovation"** — Agrawal et al., 2024 (arXiv: 2412.17866)
  - AI-assisted researchers discover 44% more materials → 39% increase in patent filings → 17% rise in downstream product innovation
  - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.17866

- **BCG (2024)**: "As GenAI Revolutionizes Product Discovery, Brands Need to Adapt"
  - Customer product discovery via GenAI is the top-ranked use case for 2025
  - 58% of Gen Z shoppers already use AI when shopping
  - Source: https://www.bcg.com/publications/2024/brands-must-adapt-as-genai-evolves-product-discovery

### Research Gaps — Discovery
- Limited validation of LLM-generated insights against ground truth customer data
- Ethical concerns around synthetic user research replacing real user voices
- Lack of standardized evaluation metrics for AI-generated customer insights

## 3. Research Area 2: AI for Product Design & Prototyping

### 3.1 Generative AI in Design

**Key Papers & Resources:**
- **"Product Design and Development Using AI Techniques: A Review"** — ResearchGate, 2023
  - Comprehensive review of AI techniques (ML, DL, NLP, computer vision) applied to product design
  - Source: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/370084641

- **"Generative AI in Knowledge Work: Design Implications for Data Navigation and Decision-Making"** — arXiv, 2025
  - Source: https://arxiv.org/html/2503.18419v1

### 3.2 AI-Driven UX & Adaptive Interfaces

**Industry Trends (2025-2026):**
- **73% of designers** say AI as a design collaborator will have the most impact in 2026
- **Generative UI**: AI creates fully dynamic interfaces based on user intent and context
- **Always-on research**: continuous insight gathering throughout product development cycles
- **54% of designers** report clients want to adopt AI trends without clear use cases — biggest gap identified

**Key Sources:**
- NN/g — "State of UX 2026: Design Deeper to Differentiate": https://www.nngroup.com/articles/state-of-ux-2026/
- Designlab — "The State of AI in UX & Product Design: 2026": https://designlab.com/blog/ai-in-ux-product-design-trends-2026/
- Grazitti — "10 AI-Powered Product Design Trends for 2026": https://www.grazitti.com/blog/the-next-wave-ai-first-product-design-trends-for-2026/

### 3.3 Trust in AI-Designed Experiences

- Trust is identified as a major design problem for AI experiences
- Four fundamentals: transparency, control, consistency, and support when systems fail
- By 2026, 80%+ organizations will use generative AI; focus shifts to integration quality and leadership

### Research Gaps — Design
- Limited academic research on AI-generated UI evaluation methodologies
- Lack of frameworks for measuring AI design quality vs. human design quality
- Generative UI is mostly industry-driven; minimal peer-reviewed research

## 4. Research Area 3: AI for Product Development & Engineering

### 4.1 AI in New Product Development (NPD)

**Key Papers:**
- **"Where Does AI Play a Major Role in NPD and Product Management?"** — Springer, Management Review Quarterly, 2025
  - Systematic literature review of 190 publications + expert interviews
  - Maps AI methods to NPD phases: sentiment analysis, knowledge extraction, demand forecasting dominate early phases
  - Later stages (testing, validation, post-launch optimization) significantly under-researched
  - Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11301-025-00533-5

- **"When Generative AI Meets Product Development"** — MIT Sloan Management Review
  - GenAI is transforming NPD but managers lack concrete integration guidance
  - Source: https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/when-generative-ai-meets-product-development/

- **"Your Synthetic Teammate: Enriching NPD with Generative AI"** — ScienceDirect, Business Horizons, 2025
  - Proposes GenAI as an NPD team member
  - Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0007681325000357

- **"AI and New Product Development"** — ResearchGate, 2024
  - Systematic review of 53 peer-reviewed articles (2022–2024) + 218 AI models
  - Introduces the "AI for Innovation" diagram — conceptual framework aligning AI capabilities with innovation phases
  - Source: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/377460646

### 4.2 AI Impact on Firm Growth

- **"Artificial Intelligence, Firm Growth, and Product Innovation"** — Journal of Financial Economics (ScienceDirect), 2023
  - AI strongly associated with higher firm growth, mainly through product innovation
  - Early adopters: up to 50% reduction in NPD time
  - Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304405X2300185X

### 4.3 Practical Applications

- **Deloitte**: "From Concept to Market: How AI Can Accelerate Physical Product Innovation"
  - Companies using AI in product development: 20-40% time-to-market reduction, 20-30% cost reduction
  - Source: https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/emerging-technologies/gen-ai-industry-product-innovation.html

### Research Gaps — Development
- AI application in concept testing stage is significantly under-researched
- No integrative AI solutions spanning multiple NPD phases end-to-end
- Absence of systematic frameworks for AI-driven product management
- Limited empirical studies on AI's impact on software product development quality

## 5. Research Area 4: AI for Product Management & Decision Making

### 5.1 AI-Powered PM Tools & Agentic AI

- Analysis of 51 AI-powered PM tools reveals only ~25% have meaningful agentic capabilities
- Agentic AI is the **least mature** dimension across the PM tool ecosystem
- Transition: Copilot AI → Agentic AI (2026-2030)
- Source: https://aipmtools.org/articles/future-of-ai-product-management

### 5.2 Key Capabilities

| Capability | Description |
|-----------|------------|
| Real-time predictive insights | Replacing intuition and historical data with AI-driven predictions |
| Unstructured data analysis | NLP for user interviews, support tickets, social media feedback |
| Automated prioritization | AI-assisted roadmap and backlog prioritization |
| Autonomous decision-making | Gartner: 15% of daily work decisions will be made by agentic AI by 2028 |

### 5.3 Agentic AI in Product Workflows

- **Gartner (2025)**: 40% of enterprise apps will feature task-specific AI agents by end of 2026 (up from <5% in 2025)
  - Source: https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025
- **Deloitte**: Agentic AI strategy — from pilot to production
  - Source: https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/technology-management/tech-trends/2026/agentic-ai-strategy.html
- AI agent market: $7.84B (2025) → $52.62B (2030), CAGR 46.3%
- Low-code/no-code platforms: agents deployable in 15-60 minutes

### 5.4 HBR & Industry Perspectives

- **HBR (2026)**: "To Drive AI Adoption, Build Your Team's Product Management Skills"
  - Source: https://hbr.org/2026/02/to-drive-ai-adoption-build-your-teams-product-management-skills
- **McKinsey State of AI 2025**: 78% of organizations reported using AI in 2024, up from 55% in 2023
  - Source: https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai

### Research Gaps — Management
- Agentic PM capabilities are least mature and under-studied
- 39.7% of product professionals cite AI-generated insight quality as their biggest concern
- Limited academic research on AI-augmented product decision-making effectiveness
- No standardized benchmarks for measuring AI PM tool performance

## 6. Research Area 5: AI for Product Growth & Lifecycle Management

### 6.1 Product Lifecycle Management (PLM)

**Key Papers:**
- **"Artificial Intelligence in Product Lifecycle Management"** — Springer, International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology, 2021
  - Comprehensive review of AI applications in PLM
  - Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00170-021-06882-1

- **"AI-Driven Process Automation in PLM: A Transformative Approach"** — ResearchGate, 2025
  - ML-based process automation, advanced analytics for decision support
  - Source: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/393318511

- **Siemens Global Survey (2026)**: AI Impact on PLM
  - Source: https://blogs.sw.siemens.com/teamcenter-manufacturing/2026/03/04/ai-impact-on-product-lifecycle-management-global-survey/

### 6.2 Digital Twins + Generative AI

- Convergence of digital twins and GenAI creates adaptive, simulation-driven design environments
- Model real-world behavior → generate optimized design alternatives → reduce prototyping costs
- Global PLM market: $53.9B (2023), CAGR 12.6%

### 6.3 AI for Personalization & Growth

- AI-driven recommendation engines analyze customer preferences and behaviors
- A/B testing automation and experimentation platforms increasingly AI-powered
- Real-time personalization at scale across product experiences

### 6.4 Industry Case Studies

| Company | AI Application | Impact |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| Mondelez | AI for snack recipe creation (flavor, cost, nutrition analysis) | 4-5x faster recipe development |
| BMW | AI in vehicle assembly quality control | >$1M annual savings |
| Netflix | AI for user experience and content recommendation | Improved engagement |
| PepsiCo | AI for product shape and flavor optimization (Cheetos) | Precise product characteristic control |
| Grammarly | GenAI writing intelligence platform | 30M DAU, $400M ARR |

### Research Gaps — Growth & Lifecycle
- Limited research on AI for post-launch product optimization
- Digital twin applications mostly in manufacturing; under-explored in software products
- AI experimentation platform research is mostly industry-driven, not academic

## 7. Cross-Cutting Themes

### 7.1 Human-AI Collaboration in Product Teams

**Key Papers:**
- **"Enhancing Human-AI Collaboration and Trust in NPD from Organization Clustering Perspective"** — ScienceDirect, Computers & Industrial Engineering, 2025
  - Trust in AI is multidimensional (technical/cognitive, emotional, social) and evolves dynamically

- **"Complementarity in Human-AI Collaboration: Concept, Sources, and Evidence"** — Taylor & Francis, European Journal of Information Systems, 2025
  - Complementary team performance (human + AI > either alone) has rarely been observed empirically
  - Source: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/0960085X.2025.2475962

- **"Measuring the Impact of Human-AI Collaboration on Knowledge Diffusion in NPD Projects"** — Springer, Engineering Management, 2025

  - Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s42524-025-4210-3

- **Microsoft New Future of Work Report 2025**

  - Source: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/New-Future-Of-Work-Report-2025.pdf

  - Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43681-023-00330-4

### 7.3 AI Frameworks & Taxonomies

- **OECD Sectoral Taxonomy of AI Intensity (2024)** — Complementarity measure for AI exposure and productivity

## 8. Research Gap Summary & Future Directions

### Consolidated Research Gaps

| # | Gap Area | Description | Severity |
|---|---------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | **End-to-End AI Integration** | No integrative AI framework spanning all product lifecycle phases | High |
| 2 | **NPD Later Stages** | AI in concept testing, product validation, and post-launch optimization under-studied | High |
| 3 | **Agentic AI for PM** | Least mature capability; limited academic research on autonomous product decisions | High |
| 4 | **Human-AI Complementarity** | Rarely achieved empirically; insufficient understanding of when/how it works | Medium |
| 5 | **AI Insight Quality** | 39.7% of professionals cite quality concerns; no standardized evaluation metrics | Medium |
| 6 | **Synthetic User Research Validity** | Ethical and methodological concerns around replacing real users with AI personas | Medium |
| 7 | **Generative UI Research** | Mostly industry-driven; minimal peer-reviewed academic work | Medium |
| 8 | **Software Product PLM** | Digital twin and PLM research concentrated in manufacturing, not software products | Low |
| 9 | **AI Design Evaluation** | No established frameworks for comparing AI-generated vs. human-generated designs | Medium |
| 10 | **Cross-Industry Benchmarks** | Lack of standardized benchmarks for AI PM tool effectiveness | Low |

### Promising Research Directions

1. **Agentic AI for Full-Lifecycle Product Management** — Developing autonomous AI agents that can manage multi-phase product workflows from discovery to optimization
2. **Human-AI Teaming Models for Product Teams** — Understanding complementarity conditions and trust dynamics in AI-augmented product teams
3. **AI-Driven Product Experimentation** — Automated hypothesis generation, experiment design, and causal inference for product decisions
4. **Responsible AI Product Design Frameworks** — Integrating ethics-by-design principles into AI-powered product development processes
5. **LLM-Based Product Intelligence** — Using foundation models for customer insight synthesis, competitive analysis, and product strategy
6. **Generative UI/UX Systems** — Academic study of dynamically generated interfaces and their impact on user experience
7. **AI for Product Quality Assurance** — Automated product testing, validation, and quality prediction across the development lifecycle
