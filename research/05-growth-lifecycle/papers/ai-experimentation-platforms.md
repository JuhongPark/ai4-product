# AI-Driven Product Experimentation & Causal Inference

> Addressing the research gap: AI experimentation platform research is mostly industry-driven, not academic

## Academic Research

### Stanford Causal AI Lab (SCAIL)
- Source: https://scail.stanford.edu/
- Developing data analytic tools for experimentation in digital platforms
- Research on estimation of heterogeneous effects from large-scale A/B tests
- New book: "Applied Causal Inference Powered by ML and AI" (Chernozhukov, Hansen, Kallus & Spindler)

### InferenceEvolve: Automated Causal Effect Estimators
**"InferenceEvolve: Automated Causal Effect Estimators through Self-Evolving AI"**
— arXiv:2604.04274, April 2026
- Source: https://arxiv.org/html/2604.04274
- LLMs help explore structured methodological spaces for causal inference
- Builds on evolutionary program synthesis
- Shows LLMs can navigate complex estimation landscapes that traditionally required expert manual iteration

### AI-Powered A/B Testing Evolution
- Source: https://blog.growthbook.io/a-b-testing-in-the-age-of-ai/

**Current AI capabilities in A/B testing:**
| Capability | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Hypothesis Generation | AI generates stronger test hypotheses from behavioral data |
| Adaptive Experiments | Real-time allocation adjustments based on interim results |
| Automated Analysis | AI interprets metrics, detects significance, communicates results |
| Personalization | Experiments converge with real-time personalization at scale |
| Causal Inference | Beyond A/B: AI identifies causal mechanisms in observational data |

### Key Trend: Experimentation → Personalization Convergence
- AI A/B testing becoming adaptive and real-time
- Traditional sequential testing being replaced by continuous optimization
- AI enables "always-on experimentation" rather than discrete test periods

## Research Gaps

- **Academic-Industry Gap**: Most advanced experimentation tools are proprietary (Google, Netflix, Amazon); limited peer-reviewed evaluation
- **Causal AI for Product Decisions**: Emerging but not yet systematically applied to product management
- **Heterogeneous Treatment Effects**: Estimating personalized effects from experiments at scale remains technically challenging
- **Long-term Experimentation**: Most AI-driven experiments optimize for short-term metrics; long-term product health effects under-studied
