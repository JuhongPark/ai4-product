# AI Governance Priorities & Frameworks: 2026 Update

## Key Source 1

**"Six AI Governance Priorities for 2026"** — Partnership on AI, 2026
- Source: https://partnershiponai.org/resource/six-ai-governance-priorities/

### The Six Priorities

1. **Establish Foundational Infrastructure to Govern AI Agents**
   - Agentic systems pose risks: irreversible actions, open-ended decision-making, privacy vulnerabilities
   - Create transparency standards and secure protocols (e.g., MCP) for inter-agent interactions
   - Develop monitoring methods with privacy protections; identify agent-specific failure modes

2. **Strengthen Documentation and Public Reporting Mechanisms**
   - Information flow across AI value chain remains fragmented
   - Coordinate documentation across value-chain layers (compute, cloud, model, deployment)
   - Strengthen international frameworks like the Hiroshima AI Process (HAIP)

3. **Coordinate Internationally Through Shared Baselines**
   - Proliferating governance initiatives lack convergence pathways
   - Focus on concrete problems like cross-border agent deployment
   - Establish mutual recognition processes for certifications and audits

4. **Preserve Human Voice and Epistemic Integrity**
   - Distinguishing authentic human creation from AI-generated content increasingly difficult
   - Invest in content credentialing beyond detection tools
   - Explore economic models supporting media viability

5. **Advance Public Understanding of AI and Workforce Resilience**
   - Policy decisions lack rigorous data on specific AI task capabilities
   - Quantify task-level capabilities across sectors and job categories
   - Promote "assurance literacy" for evaluating AI outputs

6. **Clarify AI Sovereignty Goals**
   - Balance infrastructure costs, partnerships, and dependencies
   - Map AI supply chains and dependencies (minerals, energy, models)
   - Ensure public participation in governance decisions

## Key Source 2

**Stanford HAI AI Index Report 2026** (Release: April 13, 2026)
- Source: https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index-report

### Key Findings
- **Regulatory fragmentation accelerating**: 47 countries with active AI legislation, only 12 with enforcement mechanisms
- Compliance costs vary **8x between jurisdictions**
- EU AI Act entered full enforcement January 2026; US continues sectoral approach
- **>60% of AI researchers** believe lack of interpretability is a major barrier to adoption
- **<5% of researchers** believe AGI is achievable in the short term
- **>70% of advanced AI compute** controlled by fewer than 10 companies (US + China)
