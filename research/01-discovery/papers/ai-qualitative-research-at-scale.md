# AI-Powered Qualitative Customer Research at Scale

## Key Paper

**"How AI Helps Scale Qualitative Customer Research"** — Korst, Puntoni & Toubia, Harvard Business Review, April 2026
- Source: https://hbr.org/2026/04/how-ai-helps-scale-qualitative-customer-research

## Core Argument

Large language models enable companies to overcome the traditional research tradeoff between depth and scale. Surveys offer breadth but lack nuance; interviews provide richness but limited scope. AI moderators combine both advantages.

## Key Findings

### Scale Achievement
- **Anthropic**: 80,000+ interviews across 159 countries in 70 languages
- **Microsoft**: 250+ interviews using Listen Labs, reducing timelines from weeks to days
- **Sweetgreen**: Research at one-third cost with five times more responses

### Depth of Engagement
- Verbal responses to AI interviewers are **7x longer** than typed survey responses on average
- Participants disclose more openly to AI than human interviewers, especially on sensitive topics (health, personal matters)

### Unique Advantages
1. **Sensitive Topics**: Greater disclosure to AI than human interviewers
2. **Hard-to-Reach Populations**: Healthcare professionals and busy professionals can respond asynchronously
3. **Behavioral Insights**: Video-enabled platforms capture non-verbal cues alongside verbal responses

## AI Research Platform Ecosystem (2026)

- **Listen Labs**: AI-led interviews with automated analysis — used by Microsoft
- **Synthetic Users**: LLM-powered synthetic participant platform (parity scores 85-92% vs. organic participants)
- AI-powered platforms now reduce qualitative analysis time by up to 50%
