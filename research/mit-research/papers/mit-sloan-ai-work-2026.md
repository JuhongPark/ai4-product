# MIT Sloan: AI and Work in 2026

## Source

**"Looking ahead at AI and work in 2026"** — MIT Sloan, 2026
- Source: https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/looking-ahead-ai-and-work-2026

## Key Faculty Predictions

### 1. Human-LLM Accuracy Gap (Rama Ramakrishnan)
- Shift from comparing LLMs against perfect accuracy → comparing against **human accuracy**
- Example: Humans achieve ~95% on enterprise tasks; LLMs at ~90% may surpass humans as models improve
- This reframing could affect employment calculations across multiple sectors in 2026

### 2. AI Governance Crisis (Barbara Wixom & Nick van der Meulen)
- "The old governance playbooks are not working for AI"
- Rapid technological change outpaces existing compliance and ethics frameworks
- Companies need new approaches to safely deploy AI while maintaining standards

### 3. Cognitive Atrophy Risk (Roberto Rigobon)
- Outsourcing creative thinking to AI could diminish human cognitive abilities through **neural plasticity loss**
- Similar to how GPS usage affects navigation skills
- Fundamental question for entrepreneurship and artistic endeavors

### 4. Mechanistic Interpretability (Melissa Webster)
- Research to understand AI inner workings critical for both safety and user decision-making
- Could improve how humans interact with and trust AI outputs

### 5. Experimentation → Scale (George Westerman)
- 2026 marks the shift from AI experimentation to deploying **viable, value-creating solutions at scale**
- Organizations moving past pilots toward production deployment

### 6. LLM-ification of Data (Harang Ju)
- "LLM-ification of data" as a primary 2026 trend
- Private company databases becoming accessible to **LLM-based agents** rather than solely human interfaces
- Transforms how organizations access and use their own data

- Source: https://news.mit.edu/2026/helping-ai-agents-search-to-get-best-results-from-llms-0205
