## 1. Executive Summary

MIT SDM (System Design and Management) is a graduate program jointly run by MIT School of Engineering and MIT Sloan, focusing on systems thinking, product architecture, and engineering leadership. This deep-dive identified **20+ SDM and SDM-adjacent theses** directly related to AI applications in the product lifecycle.

### Key Findings

- **Strongest coverage**: AI for engineering/development (GenAI in SDLC, LLM agents, BERT for systems engineering), digital twins (3 theses), AI strategy/management frameworks (4 theses), multi-agent/agentic AI
- **Emerging focus**: Generative multi-agent systems for collective intelligence (CAS 2025 Best Paper), GenAI tools in software development lifecycle (2024)
- **Notable gaps**: No SDM thesis on AI ethics in product design, A/B testing/experimentation, or AI-automated UX research
- **Systems thinking connection**: SDM's system architecture framework (Crawley-Cameron-Selva) provides a unique lens for structuring AI-enabled product systems

## 3. SDM Theses: AI for Design & Prototyping

### "Ship Design through Axiomatic Design Approach, Sustainable Engineering Principles and AI Methods"
- **Author**: Georgios Fardelas
- **Year**: 2021
- **Dept**: Mechanical Engineering + **SDM**
- **Advisor**: Sang-Gook Kim
- **URL**: https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/139421
- **Summary**: Integrates Bayesian ML with Axiomatic Design for ship propulsion system architecture. Uses ML to assess sustainability objectives and optimize design choices.
- **Mapping**: Design & Prototyping

### "The Uncanny Valley: An Empirical Study on Human Perceptions of AI-Generated Text and Images"
- **Author**: Deepali Kishnani
- **Year**: 2025
- **Dept**: EECS + **SDM**
- **Advisor**: Juanjuan Zhang
- **URL**: https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/159096
- **Summary**: Studies the uncanny valley effect in AI-generated content (GPT-4 text, AI images). Found 60% of participants mistook humans for AI and 40% mistook AI for humans. Critical implications for AI-generated product content and interfaces.
- **Mapping**: Design & Prototyping, Cross-cutting (Human-AI Interaction)

### Related (non-SDM): "Generative Design Tools: Implications on Design Process, Designer Behavior, and Design Outcomes"
- **Author**: Jana I. Saadi
- **Year**: 2024
- **Dept**: Mechanical Engineering
- **Advisor**: Maria C. Yang
- **URL**: https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/153664
- **Summary**: PhD thesis on how designers use generative design tools. Tool-assisted designs showed greater aesthetic variety while non-tool designs achieved higher quantitative performance.
- **Mapping**: Design & Prototyping

### Related (non-SDM): "Design Knowledge Base Using Natural Language Processing"
- **Author**: J. Gammack
- **Year**: 2022
- **Dept**: Mechanical Engineering
- **URL**: https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/144555
- **Summary**: Applies NLP to large corpora of technical design documentation to enable semantic search for information retrieval in design.
- **Mapping**: Design & Prototyping

## 5. SDM Theses: AI for Management & Decision Making

### "Designing Generative Multi-Agent Systems for Collective Intelligence and Resilience"
- **Author**: Nguyen Luc Dao
- **Year**: 2025
- **Dept**: **SDM**
- **Advisor**: Bryan R. Moser
- **URL**: https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/162506
- **Summary**: Studies how architectural choices (group size, prompting strategies, collaboration topologies) affect multi-agent LLM systems. Found Group Chat topology highly susceptible to interference while Reflexion, Crowdsourcing, and Blackboard topologies offered better resilience. Won **CAS 2025 Best Student Paper Award**.
- **Mapping**: Management & Decision Making (Agentic AI)

### "Deep Pockets: The Economics of Deep Learning and the Emergence of New AI Platforms"
- **Author**: Nicholas J. Borge
- **Year**: 2022
- **Dept**: **SDM**
- **Advisor**: Neil C. Thompson
- **URL**: https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/144985
- **Summary**: Demonstrates that net benefit of deep learning can be calculated a priori using scaling laws. Identifies economic thresholds where AI investments become profitable and how this drives market concentration and platform emergence.
- **Mapping**: Management & Decision Making

### "A Strategic Perspective on the Commercialization of Artificial Intelligence"
- **Author**: Siddhartha Ray Barua
- **Year**: 2019
- **Dept**: **SDM**
- **Advisor**: Donna H. Rhodes
- **URL**: https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/122431

- **Mapping**: Management & Decision Making

### "The Road to Enterprise Artificial Intelligence: A Case Studies Driven Exploration"

### "A Guiding Framework for Applying Machine Learning in Organizations"

### Related: "End-to-End Artificial Intelligence Lifecycle Management"

### Coverage by ai4-product Area

| ai4-product Area | SDM Thesis Count | Coverage Level | Key Theses |
|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery & User Research | 2 (+ 1 related) | Partial | Thobani (2018), Adam (2018) |
| Design & Prototyping | 2 (+ 2 related) | Moderate | Fardelas (2021), Kishnani (2025) |
| Development & Engineering | 6 | **Strong** | Prakash (2024), Lim (2022), Cao (2024), Chen (2021), Ye (2018), Peters (2025) |
| Management & Decision Making | 5 (+ 1 related) | **Strong** | Dao (2025), Borge (2022), Ray Barua (2019), Siddique (2018), Tham (2016) |
| Growth & Lifecycle | 6 | **Strong** | LeBlanc (2020), Zhao (2023), Heilbrun (2020), Asa (2020), Nakajima (2023) |
| Cross-cutting | 4 | Moderate | Thomas (2015), Liu (2022), Kalra (2023), Prestidge (2022) |

### Notable Gaps
- **AI ethics in product design**: No SDM-specific thesis
- **A/B testing / experimentation platforms**: No SDM thesis
- **AI-automated UX research / synthetic user research**: No SDM thesis
- **Recommendation engine architecture**: Only tangentially covered (Thobani 2018)

### Temporal Distribution
- 2015-2018: 5 theses (foundational frameworks, early ML applications)
- 2019-2021: 6 theses (digital twins, AI commercialization, document processing)
- 2022-2023: 6 theses (LLMs, digital twins, economics of AI, cybersecurity)
- 2024-2025: 4 theses (GenAI in SDLC, LLM agents, multi-agent systems, uncanny valley)
- **Trend**: Clear shift from foundational ML → LLM/GenAI applications, and from individual AI tools → multi-agent/agentic systems
