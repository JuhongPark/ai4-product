# Key MIT Researcher Profiles: AI for Product

> Date: 2026-03-08
> Part of: AI for Product Research Survey Project

---

## MIT Sloan School of Management

### Danielle Li
- **Position**: Associate Professor of Management
- **Research Areas**: Impact of AI on labor productivity, organizational AI adoption
- **Key Publication**: "Generative AI at Work" (QJE, 2025) — empirical evidence of 14% productivity improvement
- **Co-authors**: Erik Brynjolfsson (Stanford), Lindsey Raymond

### Thomas Malone
- **Position**: Patrick J. McGovern Professor, Founding Director of CCI
- **Research Areas**: Collective intelligence, human-AI collaboration, Superminds
- **Key Contributions**: Framework for maximizing collective intelligence through human-AI role allocation

### Andrew McAfee
- **Position**: Principal Research Scientist, Co-founder of MIT IDE
- **Research Areas**: AI organizational theory, data-driven management, digital transformation
- **Key Publication**: "The Geek Way" — organizational culture of experiment- and data-driven decision-making

### Sinan Aral
- **Position**: David Austin Professor, Director of MIT IDE
- **Research Areas**: Digital experimentation (A/B testing), social networks, causal inference
- **Key Contributions**: Host of MIT CODE Conference, Pareto Machines project (MGAIC)
- **URL**: https://www.sinanaral.io/

### Kate Kellogg
- **Position**: David J. McGrath Jr. Professor of Management
- **Research Areas**: Agentic AI implementation, technological change in organizations
- **Key Contributions**: Discovery that 80% of agentic AI work is "invisible work"

### Dean Eckles
- **Position**: Associate Professor, KDD Professor of Marketing
- **Research Areas**: Causal inference, social networks, large-scale online experimentation
- **Key Contributions**: Network interference problems, AI-based causal inference methodology

### Duncan Simester
- **Position**: NTU Professor of Marketing
- **Research Areas**: Machine learning-based experimental design, customer behavior prediction, heterogeneous treatment effects
- **Key Contributions**: Large-scale field experiment optimization, personalization strategies

### Catherine Tucker
- **Position**: Sloan Distinguished Professor of Management
- **Research Areas**: AI privacy, data policy, digital advertising
- **Key Contributions**: Empirical research on the impact of regulations such as GDPR on AI products

### Retsef Levi
- **Position**: J. Spencer Standish Professor
- **Research Areas**: AI supply chain risk, operations optimization
- **Key Contributions**: MGAIC "GenAI for Software Supply Chain Risk Management"

### John Hauser
- **Position**: Kirin Professor of Marketing (Emeritus)
- **Research Areas**: Voice of the Customer, product design, customer needs extraction
- **Key Contributions**: Pioneer of customer needs analysis frameworks

### Alexandre Jacquillat
- **Position**: Associate Professor of Operations Management
- **Research Areas**: Optimization, transportation/logistics, AI decision-making
- **Key Contributions**: MGAIC "LLM-based Transportation/Logistics Decision Model"

- **Position**: Professor, EECS

- **Position**: Professor, EECS
- **Awards**: ACM SIGSOFT Outstanding Research Award (2025)
- **Research Areas**: Program Repair, Approximate Computing, LLM program semantics

### Daniel Jackson
- **Position**: CSAIL Associate Director, Professor of CS, ACM Fellow

- **Awards**: IEEE Edison Medal (2025)
- **Research Areas**: Robotics, manufacturing automation, Robot Compiler
- **Key Contributions**: System enabling non-experts to design and fabricate custom robots — democratizing manufacturing through AI

### Srini Devadas
- **Position**: Professor, EECS
- **Research Areas**: Computer security, cryptography, AI privacy

## MIT Economics

### Daron Acemoglu
- **Position**: MIT Institute Professor
- **Awards**: 2024 Nobel Prize in Economics
- **Research Areas**: AI macroeconomics, automation vs. augmentation
- **Key Contributions**: Predicted AI will increase GDP by only 1.1-1.6%; argued for focusing on augmenting rather than replacing humans

### David Autor
- **Position**: EECS Professor, 2024 AI2050 Senior Fellow
- **Research Areas**: AI and the future of work, middle-skill labor markets
- **Key Contributions**: AI simultaneously devalues and amplifies expertise; collaborative design can restore the middle class

### Sendhil Mullainathan
- **Position**: Peter de Florez Professor (Economics + EECS); returned to MIT in 2024
- **Research Areas**: AI fairness, algorithmic bias, behavioral economics
- **Key Contributions**: "The Bike Shop @ MIT" research center; using AI to uncover biases in human decision-making

## MIT LIDS

### Devavrat Shah
- **Position**: Andrew and Erna Viterbi Professor of EECS
- **Awards**: ACM SIGMETRICS Achievement Award (2025)
- **Research Areas**: Recommender system theory, causal inference, misinformation detection algorithms
- **Key Contributions**: First to provide theoretical performance guarantees for recommender systems under sparse sampling conditions
