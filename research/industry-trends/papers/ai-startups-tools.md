## 1. AI Product Management Tools

### Productboard — Spark
- **Productboard Spark** (October 2025): Specialized product agent with "context-native intelligence" — knows product landscape, provides evidence-backed guidance across discovery, competitive analysis, spec writing, and launch orchestration
- Shift from feedback aggregation to agentic "product co-pilot" with domain-specific training
- Backed by Dragoneer, Tiger Global, Index Ventures, Kleiner Perkins, Sequoia, Bessemer

### Amplitude — Agentic Analytics
- **Automated Insights** (December 2025): Autonomous AI investigating metric changes without human intervention
- **Agentic AI Analytics** (February 2026): Global Agent + four specialized agents pushing data into Anthropic, Cursor, Figma, Lovable, Notion, GitHub
- **Ask Amplitude**: NLP + vector search for natural language querying
- **AI Visibility** (October 2025): Track brand presence in AI search results
- First major analytics platform to ship fully autonomous analytics agent pipeline

### Pendo — AI Intelligence Layer
- **Pendo AI / Pendo Insights**: ML-driven segmentation, in-product usage correlation, churn prediction, adoption tracking
- Tight coupling of behavioral analytics, in-app guides, and NPS feedback

### Market Context
- Product teams spend 66%+ of their week on manual work — primary driver of PM AI tool adoption
- Average time saved per task: **4 hours**; ~33 hours total across core PM functions
- 100% of product teams surveyed use AI tools; 96% consistently

## 2. AI Design Tools

### Figma Make (May 2025)
- Prompt-to-app/prototype tool ingesting Figma design libraries with backend integration (e.g., Supabase)
- **Model Context Protocol (MCP)**: Direct data pipeline feeding design tokens into AI coding assistants
- Dev Mode evolved into AI-powered code co-pilot
- 23% of designers/developers say most of their work is now on AI-powered products (up from 17%)
- 33% use AI for design asset generation; 22% for first-draft interfaces

### Galileo AI → Google Stitch (May 2025)
- Acquired by Google; relaunched as **Google Stitch** powered by Gemini
- Prompt-based multi-screen UI generation, image-to-UI transformation, production-ready export
- Pricing shifted from $39/month → **free** post-acquisition

### Framer AI
- Full prompt-to-website generation; "Wireframer" for responsive layout generation (Spring 2025)
- Workshop (AI component/animation generation), AI localization, on-page editing
- **$100M Series D at $2B valuation** (August 2025); $163M total raised

### Uizard
- Sketch/wireframe-to-interactive-UI-prototype conversion
- AI color scheme generation, attention heatmap predictor
- Focused on earliest ideation phase; accessible to non-designers

## Cross-Cutting Trends

### 1. Consolidation via Acquisition (2023-2025)
Four major exits signal market maturation:
- Statsig → OpenAI ($1.1B)
- Eppo → Datadog (~$220M)
- Galileo AI → Google (Stitch)
- Heap → Contentsquare

### 2. Copilot → Agentic Transition
Every category shifting from "AI-assisted" to "AI-autonomous" — agents independently running experiments, moderating interviews, writing specs, generating full apps.

### 3. Vibe Coding Explosion
AI development platforms saw the most dramatic growth:
- Cursor ($29.3B), Lovable ($6.6B), Replit ($9B) — collectively >$45B in valuations
- Companies that barely existed 3 years ago

### 4. Synthetic Research Rise
Aaru ($1B valuation), Blok, Artificial Societies represent a new category raising questions about when synthetic participants can substitute for human research.

### 5. Platform Convergence
Analytics (Mixpanel, Amplitude), experimentation (Statsig, Eppo), and feature flags converging. PostHog's "all-in-one" approach forcing incumbents to expand scope.

### 6. Open-Source as Strategy
PostHog and GrowthBook use open-source cores for developer trust and viral distribution — 65% of YC companies use PostHog.
