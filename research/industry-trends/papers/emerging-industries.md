### Chegg (Cautionary Tale)

**CheggMate (GPT-4 powered) --- Disruption from Below**
- Launched GPT-4-powered **CheggMate** in 2023 as an AI study companion
- Initial success: 31% improvement in 30-day conversion rate; 25% QoQ increase in Study Pack subscribers (Q4 2024)
- **Then the floor fell out**: Q1 2025 revenue down **30% YoY** to $121.4M; subscribers dropped **31% YoY** to 3.2 million
- October 2025: **slashed 45% of workforce**, explicitly blaming "new realities of AI"
- Root cause: Free AI alternatives (ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Claude) eliminated Chegg's core value proposition of homework answers

| Metric | Q1 2024 | Q1 2025 | Change |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| Revenue | ~$173M | $121.4M | -30% |
| Subscribers | ~4.6M | 3.2M | -31% |
| Workforce | ~100% | ~55% | -45% (Oct 2025 layoffs) |

**Lesson**: Chegg's AI product (CheggMate) could not defend against commoditized AI. When the core value proposition (answers to homework) becomes freely available through general-purpose AI, a domain-specific AI wrapper adds insufficient differentiation.

Sources: [Chegg Q1 2025 Revenue](https://www.edtechinnovationhub.com/news/chegg-q1-2025-revenue-down-30-as-company-restructures-and-explores-strategic-alternatives), [Chegg Layoffs (CNBC)](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/27/chegg-slashes-45percent-of-workforce-blames-new-realities-of-ai.html), [Chegg Q4 2024 Results](https://investor.chegg.com/Press-Releases/press-release-details/2025/Chegg-Reports-2024-Fourth-Quarter-and-Full-Year-Financial-Results/default.aspx)

### Babylon Health (Post-Mortem)

**AI Healthcare Chatbot --- Collapsed 2023**
- Once valued at **$4.2 billion**; went public via SPAC
- US branch filed for **bankruptcy August 2023**; UK branch entered administration weeks later
- Core claims --- that its AI could diagnose as well as human doctors --- were **disputed by The Lancet** (November 2018) and never independently validated
- Expanded aggressively without robust risk frameworks for AI performance, bias, or liability

**Failure factors**:
1. AI accuracy claims not backed by independent clinical validation
2. Aggressive geographic/product expansion without operational maturity
3. Governance failures: no adequate explainability or bias frameworks
4. Burn rate exceeded revenue growth

**Successor**: Founder Ali Parsa launched **Qu** (November 2024), a "personal digital clinical assistant," in beta.

**Lesson**: In healthcare AI, clinical validation and regulatory trust are non-negotiable. Marketing AI capabilities ahead of evidence destroys credibility and, ultimately, the business.

Sources: [Babylon Health Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babylon_Health), [Babylon Case Study](https://cyberdoctorlalitgupta.com/case-study-babylon-health-when-ai-promises-outpace-accountability/), [Ali Parsa Launches Qu](https://www.digitalhealth.net/2024/11/babylon-founder-ali-parsa-returns-with-new-healthcare-ai-venture/)

## Cross-Cutting Patterns

### Pattern 1: AI as Product Core vs. AI as Feature

| Category | Companies | Description |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **AI-native products** | Waymo, Tempus, Viz.ai, Duolingo (Birdbrain) | AI is the product --- without AI, the product does not exist |
| **AI-enhanced products** | Procore, Coursera, Oscar Health, Autodesk | AI adds significant value but the product existed before AI |
| **AI-disrupted products** | Chegg, Babylon Health | AI (from competitors or the market) destroyed the value proposition |

**Insight**: The most defensible position is AI-native with proprietary data flywheel (Duolingo, Tempus, Waymo). AI-enhanced products must integrate deeply enough that the AI cannot be trivially replaced by a general-purpose model.

### Pattern 2: Data Flywheel as Competitive Moat

The strongest performers share a common structure:

```
More users --> More data --> Better AI --> Better product --> More users
```

### Pattern 7: Regulatory Environment as Differentiator

Industries with high regulatory barriers (healthcare, autonomous driving) create stronger AI moats:
- **Viz.ai**: 50+ FDA clearances create a barrier that general-purpose AI cannot easily cross
- **Waymo**: Years of safety data and regulatory approvals across multiple cities/states
- **Tempus**: CAP-accredited, CLIA-certified laboratory infrastructure

Conversely, industries with low regulatory barriers (EdTech, content) are more susceptible to disruption by general-purpose AI (Chegg's collapse).
