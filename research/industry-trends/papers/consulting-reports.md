## 4. Bain & Company

### Key Reports (Technology Report 2025)
- "From Pilots to Payoff: Generative AI in Software Development"
- "State of the Art of Agentic AI Transformation"

### Key Statistics

| Metric | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| Software firms deployed GenAI tools | 67% |
| Typical productivity boost (tool adoption) | 10-15% |
| Productivity boost (end-to-end transformation) | 25-30% |
| Companies citing culture/behavior change as hardest part | 75% |

### Bain's Four Levels of Agentic AI Capability
1. **Level 1**: LLM-powered information retrieval (knowledge assistants, copilots)
2. **Level 2**: Single-task agentic workflows (self-contained action loops)
3. **Level 3**: Cross-system agentic workflow orchestration (supervised)
4. **Level 4**: Multi-agent constellations (any-to-any, loosely coupled collaborative agents)

### Core Finding
The gap between 10% and 30% productivity gains is a **process transformation gap**, not a technology gap. 3 of 4 companies say the hardest part is getting people to change how they work.

### Sources
- https://www.bain.com/insights/from-pilots-to-payoff-generative-ai-in-software-development-technology-report-2025/
- https://www.bain.com/insights/state-of-the-art-of-agentic-ai-transformation-technology-report-2025/

## Universal Finding: The AI Value Gap

Every firm independently identified the same paradox — **broad adoption, narrow value capture**:

| Source | Adoption | Value Capture |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| McKinsey | 88% adoption | Only 6% are high performers |
| BCG | 75% rank AI as top priority | Only 25% achieve significant value |
| Deloitte | 74% want revenue growth from AI | Only 20% have seen it |
| Forrester | Mass investment | Only 15% report EBITDA lift |
| Bain | 67% deployed GenAI tools | 10-15% gains (vs. 25-30% with transformation) |

### Root Cause Consensus
The gap is not technological — it is **organizational**:
- Process redesign, not process automation (Deloitte)
- People and processes account for 70% of success (BCG 10-20-70 Rule)
- Culture/behavior change is the hardest part for 75% of companies (Bain)
- High performers fundamentally reworked processes at 3x the rate (McKinsey)
